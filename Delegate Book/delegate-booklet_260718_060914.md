# 01. About Duothan 6.0 

Duothan is the flagship national-level hackathon of the IEEE Student Branch of NSBM Green University, organized together with the IEEE Computer Society Chapter, the IEEE Women in Engineering Affinity Group, and the IEEE SIGHT Affinity Group of NSBM. Now in its sixth edition, Duothan 6.0 is framed as a national inter-university "DevOps Combat" designed to inspire innovation, teamwork, and engineering excellence among undergraduates across Sri Lanka.

<mark>04</mark> 







- Team Composition: Teams must consist of 2-4 registered members. Any team changes require organizer approval before registration closes; no changes are permitted afterward. 

- Original Work: All submitted work must be original and primarily developed during the competition period. 

- University Representation: Cross-university teams are not permitted; all members must represent the same university throughout. 

- Adherence to Guidelines: Teams must follow all instructions in the competition context documents so their solution aligns with the expected outcomes and evaluation criteria. 

- Strict Deadlines: All project submissions must be completed before the specified deadline. Late submissions will not be accepted. 

- Code of Conduct: Respect judges, mentors, organizers, and fellow participants. Harassment, misconduct, or disruptive behaviour may result in disqualification. 

- Zero Tolerance for Plagiarism: Plagiarism, unauthorized 

   - collaboration, or copying another team's work is strictly prohibited and will lead to immediate disqualification. 

- Data Responsibility: Teams are responsible for backing up their own project files and source code. Organizers are not liable for any data loss. 

- Final Decisions: Decisions of the judging panel and organizing committee are final and cannot be challenged or appealed. 

<mark>O5</mark> 







The judging process of Duothan 6.0 is conducted by an independent panel comprising representatives from our Knowledge Partners, industry experts, and academic professionals. The panel brings expertise in software engineering, DevOps, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and system architecture to ensure a fair and professional evaluation throughout the competition. 



- The judging panel consists of representatives from Duothan 6.0 Knowledge Partners, industry professionals, and academic experts. 

- Judges are selected based on their technical expertise and industry experience. 

- The organizing committee oversees the judging process to ensure consistency and fairness. 



- Each competition phase is evaluated independently according to its objectives and required deliverables. 

- Teams must successfully complete the requirements of the current phase to qualify for the next stage. 

- Only submissions made through the official submission channels before the deadline will be considered. 

- Judges may review source code, documentation, repositories, deployment links, and any additional materials submitted by the team. 

<mark>06</mark> 







- To maintain the integrity of the competition, judges or the organizing committee may: 

- Verify the originality of submitted work. 

- Request clarification regarding implementation decisions. 

- Verify cloud deployments, repositories, or application functionality. 

- * Confirm that all mandatory competition requirements have been satisfied. 

- Request additional evidence if a submission requires further verification. 



- Every team is evaluated under the same competition rules and 

- judging procedures. 

- Judges remain impartial throughout the competition. 

- Any conflict of interest will be managed by the organizing committee. 

- - Evaluation discussions remain confidential and are not disclosed to participants.
